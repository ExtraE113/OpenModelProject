Change the weighting method

Polls are weighted to ensure that they are representative but this weighting can be subjective. "Traditional thinking" involves weighting for factors like age, race, and gender. Recent political science research indicates that also adjusting for things like Facebook usage, social trust, and views on the bible can lead to more accurate results. Change the weighting method and see how the margins change!

Likely/registered voters

To create a view of what will happen on election day, it is important to only sample those who vote. However, many people ineligible to vote and eligible voters who end up deciding not to vote still show up in our survey sample. To resolve this, we create a probabilistic likely voter model and weigh our results accordingly.

Our likely voter model is based on the Perry-Gallup index outlined in Pew 2016. You can read more about how it works here.
